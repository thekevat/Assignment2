Analog Communication

The Analog Communication course at IET DAVV provides a strong foundation in traditional communication systems, focusing on the transmission and reception of analog signals. This course is essential for understanding the fundamentals of radio communication, broadcasting, signal processing, and telecommunication systems.

  • Introduction to Communication Systems – Basic communication model, need for modulation
  • Amplitude Modulation (AM) – DSB-FC, DSB-SC, SSB-SC, VSB, power calculations, envelope detection
  • Angle Modulation (FM & PM) – Frequency Modulation (NBFM, WBFM), Phase Modulation, Carson’s rule
  • Transmitters & Receivers – Superheterodyne receivers, frequency synthesizers, noise analysis
  • Noise in Communication Systems – Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR), noise figure, noise performance in AM and FM
  • Pulse Modulation Techniques – PAM, PWM, PPM, time-division multiplexing (TDM)
  • Multiplexing & Bandwidth Considerations – FDM, TDM, channel capacity, spectrum utilization
  • Analog to Digital Transition – Basics of PCM, quantization, introduction to digital communication
